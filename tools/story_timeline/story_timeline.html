What it does

The Story Timeline Organizer helps organize events, chapter, and books in a coherent and chronological order. It is useful for authors, writers, storyboarding, video production, and so much more. The tool is designed to help storytellers get their ideas and important events down in chronological order while being able to store ideas and events in a Parking Lot until they can be revisited. You can create different blocks (Books, Chapters, Events) and allot details to each block. You can also reorder the blocks by dragging and dropping them. Download as a JSON to revisit your project later or export your timeline as a DOCX or TXT to share with others.

How to use
1
Enter your Project Name at the top to name your story or series.
2
Use Add Book, Add Chapter, and Add Event to build out your the different parts of your timeline. You can name your blocks and drag and drop the blocks to reorder them by clicking the handle on the right side of the block.
3
You can delete items by clicking the trash icon next to them, move them to the Parking Lot for later by clicking the car icon, you can convert each block to a different type using the convert icon, and you can give each block a detail and descriptions by clicking the carrot option to reveal a details box
4
You can recover deleted items from the Recycle Bin and retrieve items from the Parking Lot at any time. Items recovered and retrieved from these places are restored to where they were before being deleted or parked.
5
When done be sure to save you work as a JSON file so you can come back and work on it later. You can also save it as a DOCX or TXT.
💡 Just because the blocks are called Book, Chapter, and Event doesn't mean that is what they have to be. If you are storyboarding a movie or play, you can use Book blocks for acts, Chapter blocks for scenes, and Event blocks for specific actions or plot points.
